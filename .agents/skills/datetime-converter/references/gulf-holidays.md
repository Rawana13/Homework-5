# Gulf Public Holidays & Business Calendar Reference

## Weekend Structure

| Country | Weekend | Workweek | Notes |
|---------|---------|----------|-------|
| Saudi Arabia | Fri + Sat | Sun–Thu | All sectors |
| Kuwait | Fri + Sat | Sun–Thu | All sectors |
| Bahrain | Fri + Sat | Sun–Thu | All sectors |
| Qatar | Fri + Sat | Sun–Thu | All sectors |
| Oman | Fri + Sat | Sun–Thu | All sectors |
| UAE (government) | Fri + Sat | Sun–Thu | Pre-2022 and some entities |
| UAE (all sectors) | **Sat + Sun** | **Mon–Fri** | Since January 2022 |

> **UAE 2022 Reform:** Effective January 1, 2022, the UAE officially shifted to a
> Monday–Friday workweek with Saturday–Sunday weekend for federal government and
> many private companies. Some private-sector companies (especially those with
> GCC clients) retained the Fri–Sat weekend. When scheduling meetings involving
> UAE parties, clarify which schedule applies or default to Mon–Fri and add a note.

## Saudi Arabia — Public Holidays

### Fixed annual holidays

| Holiday | Gregorian date | Approximate days off |
|---------|----------------|---------------------|
| **Founding Day** (يوم التأسيس) | February 22 | 1 day |
| **Saudi National Day** (اليوم الوطني) | September 23 | 1 day |

### Variable Islamic holidays (approximate Gregorian equivalents)

These shift ~11 days earlier each Gregorian year because the Hijri calendar is
shorter than the Gregorian year by 11 days.

#### Eid al-Fitr (عيد الفطر) — End of Ramadan

| Year | Approximate start | Official days off |
|------|-------------------|-------------------|
| 2024 | April 10, 2024 | 4 days (29 Ramadan – 3 Shawwal) |
| 2025 | March 30, 2025 | 4 days |
| 2026 | March 20, 2026 | 4 days |
| 2027 | March 9, 2027 | 4 days |

> Actual dates confirmed by moon sighting; may shift ±1 day.
> Saudi government typically announces holiday dates 1–2 days in advance.

#### Eid al-Adha (عيد الأضحى) — Feast of Sacrifice (10 Dhul Hijjah)

| Year | Approximate start | Official days off |
|------|-------------------|-------------------|
| 2024 | June 16, 2024 | 5 days (9–13 Dhul Hijjah) |
| 2025 | June 5, 2025 | 5 days |
| 2026 | May 26, 2026 | 5 days |
| 2027 | May 16, 2027 | 5 days |

> The period of Hajj (8–13 Dhul Hijjah) sees very reduced government activity
> even before the official holiday announcement.

#### Islamic New Year (رأس السنة الهجرية) — 1 Muharram

Saudi Arabia does **not** officially observe this as a public holiday, but
government activity is typically reduced. Banks may have limited hours.

#### Prophet's Birthday (المولد النبوي) — 12 Rabi' al-Awwal

| Year | Approximate date |
|------|-----------------|
| 2024 | September 15, 2024 |
| 2025 | September 4, 2025 |
| 2026 | August 25, 2026 |
| 2027 | August 14, 2027 |

> Saudi Arabia does **not** officially observe the Prophet's Birthday as a public
> holiday (different from most other Muslim-majority countries which do).

## Ramadan Business Hours

### Saudi Arabia

| Sector | Normal hours | Ramadan hours |
|--------|-------------|---------------|
| Government | 07:30–14:30 or 08:00–15:00 | **09:00–14:00** |
| Banks | 08:00–16:00 | **09:00–13:00** |
| Large private companies | 08:30–17:30 | **09:00–15:00** (typical) |
| Retail / malls | 10:00–22:00 | 10:00–01:00 (extended!) |

> **Note:** During Ramadan, retail activity *increases* significantly in the
> evenings after Iftar. Meetings in the 20:00–22:00 Riyadh window are
> culturally very acceptable.

### UAE

Similar to Saudi Arabia; government offices and banks shorten hours.
Private sector varies significantly by company and ownership.

## Key Scheduling Guidance

### Saudi Arabia + US combination

- **Best overlap window (standard):** 09:00–10:00 EDT = 16:00–17:00 AST
  - Only 1 hour of overlap on Mon–Thu (Gulf workweek ends Thursday;
    US workweek includes Friday but Gulf does not)
  - Friday: holiday in Saudi; regular workday in US — **no overlap**

- **Best overlap window (Ramadan):** 09:00–09:30 EDT = 16:00–16:30 AST
  - Ramadan shortened hours (09:00–14:00 AST = 06:00–11:00 UTC)
  - US hours (09:00–18:00 EDT = 13:00–22:00 UTC)
  - **No overlap during Ramadan for this city pair**
  - → Only option is very early DC (pre-09:00 EDT) or post-Iftar Riyadh (20:00+ AST)

### Saudi Arabia + UK combination

- **Best overlap window (standard, BST/summer):** 09:00–10:00 BST (UTC+1) = 11:00–12:00 AST
  - 3-hour overlap: 09:00–12:00 AST (06:00–09:00 BST / 08:00–11:00 UTC)
  - Wait — actually: Saudi (06:00–14:00 UTC) + UK BST (08:00–17:00 UTC)
  - Overlap: 08:00–14:00 UTC = 6 hours. Very comfortable window!

### Three-city combo: DC + London + Riyadh

- **Feb 2000 (EST, GMT, AST):**
  - DC: 14:00–23:00 UTC | London: 09:00–18:00 UTC | Riyadh: 06:00–14:00 UTC
  - Overlap start: max(14:00, 09:00, 06:00) = **14:00 UTC**
  - Overlap end: min(23:00, 18:00, 14:00) = **14:00 UTC**
  - **Zero overlap** — DC starts at the exact instant Riyadh ends.
  - *This is a known hard scheduling problem for this three-city combo.*

- **April 2026 (EDT, BST, AST):**
  - DC: 13:00–22:00 UTC | London: 08:00–17:00 UTC | Riyadh: 06:00–14:00 UTC
  - Overlap start: max(13:00, 08:00, 06:00) = **13:00 UTC**
  - Overlap end: min(22:00, 17:00, 14:00) = **14:00 UTC**
  - **1-hour overlap** at 13:00–14:00 UTC
    = 09:00–10:00 EDT / 14:00–15:00 BST / 16:00–17:00 AST
